I was born and raised on a farm in southeast Nebraska. After high school, I worked as a security guard and then for a Farmers Cooperative as branch manager. In 1990, I became a police officer working for Cass County Nebraska and then for LaVista Nebraska. After 30 years in law enforcement, I retired to start my counseling service.

Over the years I have volunteered as an EMT and a trained member of the Critical Incident Stress Management (CISM) for the State of Nebraska. These years of helping individuals through difficult situations lead me to my interest in counseling.

In 1998, I obtained a Bachelor of Science in Management degree from Bellevue University. I achieved my Master of Science in Social Work and Master of Science in Criminology/Criminal Justice in 2020 from University of Nebraska Omaha. I currently have a provisional license in Mental Health, PLMHP, Master of Social Work, PLMSW (June 2020) and Alcohol and Drug Counseling, PLADC (May 2021). Other accomplishments include a published article on elderly suicide, presenting a webinar for the Nebraska Chapter of National Social Workers Association on Crisis Intervention and trainer for CISM. I have been asked and have accepted as a provider for the National Fire Fighters Association and National Fraternal Order of Police.
